Full-Body Info



The Full-Body Workout maximizes your time by targeting all major muscle groups in your body.

This workout consists of the following exercises:

  • Chest Press
  • Squats
  • Row
  • Romanian Deadlift (RDL)

Sets of Chest Presses will work out your core and help to build ab muscles.

Sets of Squats will target thigh muscles to build up your lower body strength. Be much to go low to make the most of this exercise.

Rows will work to build arm and chest muscles.

Romanian Deadlifts will target the arms and chest but also will assist in building core strength.


Upper-Body Info



The Upper-Body Workout focuses on building bicep, tricep and chest strength and endurance.


This workout consists of the following exercises:

  • Chest Press
  • Bicep Curl
  • Row
  • Dips

Sets of Chest Presses will work out your core and help to build ab muscles.

Sets of bicep curls targets the aforementioned bicep muscles.

Rows will work to build arm and chest muscles.

Dips will help build your arm and chest muscles. Make sure to dip down as far you can.


Lower-Body Info



The Lower-Body Workout aims to build thigh, leg and calf strength through a series of diverse exercises.

This workout consists of the following exercises:

  • Lunges
  • Squats
  • Leg Press
  • Romanian Deadlift (RDL)

Lunges serve as a great warm up exercise for this workout to help get blood pumping to your lower body

Sets of Squats will target thigh muscles to build up your lower body strength. Be much to go low to make the most of this exercise.

Leg Presses will target your thigher and leg muscle to build a good foundation for lower body strength

Romanian Deadlifts may target the arms and chest but also will assist in building lower body strength.


Cardio Info



The Cardio Workout aims to improve your endurance, a core component to any healthy persons workout routine.


This workout consists of the following exercises:

  • 30-Minute Jog
  • 30-Minute Cycling

Jogging should help you build up anerobic endurance allowing you to build stamina which transfers over to other workouts.

Cycling is another great avenue to build anerobic strength while not being as harsh on the knees as running.

Calorie Info



The following are some tips to help you in tracking and staying within your calorie limits.

Calories are units of energy which the body either uses or stores in the body in the form of fat.


Eating less calories than what your body uses is one of the most effective ways to lose weight. Eating more calories than what the body uses will allow you to gain weight.

Most foods sold in the United States are force to list out the number of calories they contain on the packaging. Use this to make the best choice for your health.

Calorie trackers are a great way to get an idea of how many calories you consume daily and whether you need to increase or decrease intake at any point. Use our own integreated calorie tracker under the nutrition tab.


Fats Info



The following is some info surrounding fats in your food.

Fats in food are not the same fat that your body stores. Fats in food is actually helpful as they allow your body to absorb Vitamins A and D.

That being said not all fats found in your foods are beneficial for your body. Saturated and Trans fats are quite harmful to the body.

However there are plenty of fats that do benefit your body, for example omega-3 fatty acids found in fish and grains have shown benefits to the body.

Like all foods, fats are best consumed in moderation. Eating too much fat can lead to health complications down the road. Keep track of fats in your food using trackers such as the one under the nutrition tab.


Protein Info



The following is some info surrounding proteins in your food.

Proteins are crucial components in any balanced diet as they are core components used in creating muscle in the body.

Proteins are typically found in meats and in other foods such as beans and nuts.

Proteins will work to repair damaged muscles after workouts. This build the muscles back stronger and leads in an increase in strength and endurance in the long run.

While a great part of a balanced diet, proteins should not be over consumed. To ensure you do not do so, use a food tracker app such as the one under the nutrition tab in this app.


Carbs Info



The following is some info surrounding Carbohydrates in your food.

Carbohydrates or Carbs are one of the body's main sources of energy that it gets from foods.

Carbohydrates are typically found in breads and pasta and are great options in a diet where you tend to burn a lot of calories in a given day.

However in diets where you tend to not be very active, an excess of carbs can lead to issues such high blood sugar or cardiovascular issues.

To ensure that you are not over indulging in Carbs, keep track of the food you consume with a tracking app, such as the one in the nutrition tab.
